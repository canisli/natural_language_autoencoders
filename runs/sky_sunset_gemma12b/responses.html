The color of the sky at sunset is a beautiful and complex mix, but here's a breakdown of what you typically see:

*   **Reds and Oranges:** These are the most prominent colors. As the sun dips lower, sunlight has to travel through more of the Earth's atmosphere. Shorter wavelengths of light (blues and violets) are scattered away by air molecules (this is why the sky is blue during the day). Longer wavelengths (reds and oranges) are less scattered and reach our eyes.
*   **Yellows:** Often present near the sun itself.
*   **Pinks and Purples:** These can appear when there are more particles in the atmosphere (like dust, pollution, or volcanic ash) to scatter the light in different ways.
